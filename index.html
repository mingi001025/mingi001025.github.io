Mingi Kwon

I am Mingi Kwon, a Master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC San Diego (UCSD), advised by Prof. Jishen Zhao in the STABLE Lab. My research focuses on computer architecture, workload characterization, and hardware–software co-design for large-scale AI and ML systems.

I study how modern machine learning workloads stress real hardware, and how we can redesign architectures and runtime systems to make them faster and more efficient. Recently, I’ve been characterizing AlphaFold3 inference and large-scale LLM serving to identify bottlenecks across compute, memory, and interconnect. Our work on AlphaFold3 performance characterization was accepted to IEEE IISWC 2025.

Previously, I was a research intern at Samsung SAIT (Corporate R&D), where I optimized an out-of-order RISC-V CPU frontend, achieving significant area and timing improvements via microarchitectural refactoring. I am currently extending this line of work to multi-agent LLM systems, focusing on inference scalability, batching, and KV-cache behavior on modern GPU clusters.

Research

I'm interested in computer architecture, workload characterization, and efficient system design for AI. My work explores how deep learning workloads behave on modern hardware and how we can co-optimize architectures and runtime systems for performance and scalability.

AlphaFold3 Workload Characterization: A Comprehensive Analysis of Bottlenecks and Performance Scaling
Jinpyo Kim, Mingi Kwon, Jishen Zhao
IEEE International Symposium on Workload Characterization (IISWC), 2025
paper / code / slides

We build and analyze AFSysBench, a benchmarking and profiling framework for AlphaFold3 inference. We identify key bottlenecks in Evoformer-style attention, structure refinement, and diffusion-style components across heterogeneous CPU/GPU platforms. We show where compute, memory bandwidth, and interconnect become limiting, and discuss implications for future AI-centric architectures.
