I’m Mingi Kwon, a second-year M.S. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD), where I’m a member of the [STABLE Lab](https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~jzhao/index.html) advised by Prof. Jishen Zhao
.

My research focuses on computer architecture, workload characterization, and performance analysis of AI and ML systems. I’m particularly interested in bridging the gap between system-level performance profiling and architectural optimization to improve the efficiency of large-scale workloads such as AlphaFold3 and LLM serving systems.

Previously, I worked at Samsung SAIT (Corporate R&D) as a CPU Microarchitecture Intern, where I optimized the frontend of Samsung’s out-of-order RISC-V core (SRISCV project). My broader interests span hardware–software co-design, VLSI, and AI system acceleration.

Outside of research, I enjoy surfing and playing soccer. I'm grateful to be living in San Diego where sun is always shining!
